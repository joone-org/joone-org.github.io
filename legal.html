3. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) AND COUNTER TERRORISM FINANCING (CTF) POLICY

Joone is committed to complying with all applicable laws and regulations regarding Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter Terrorism Financing (CTF). This policy outlines our commitment to detect, prevent, and report money laundering and terrorist financing activities. All directors, officers, employees, and associated persons of Joone are expected to adhere to these standards to protect Joone and its operations.

Legal Framework

This policy is based on various international and local legal requirements, including but not limited to the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), the USA PATRIOT Act, and the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) recommendations. Joone's AML/CTF policy and practices are subject to all applicable laws and regulations in the jurisdictions where we operate.

AML/CTF Program

Joone will establish and maintain a risk-based AML/CTF Program which includes the following:

  • Customer Due Diligence (CDD): Identification and verification of customers' identity during the account opening process and ongoing monitoring of customer transactions to identify unusual or suspicious activities.
  • Reporting and Record Keeping: Timely reporting of suspicious transactions to the relevant authorities and maintaining appropriate records of customer identification, transactions, and reporting for a period defined by local law.
  • Training: Regular training programs for employees to understand and implement AML/CTF measures.

Customer Due Diligence

Joone is committed to understanding its customers and their financial behavior by implementing effective CDD processes. This includes:

  • Identity Verification: Collecting appropriate identification documents from customers at the time of account creation.
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Regularly reviewing customer accounts and transactions to identify unusual or suspicious behavior.

Suspicious Activity Reporting

If Joone identifies any suspicious activity, it will take appropriate steps to investigate and report such activities to the relevant authorities in accordance with local laws and regulations.

Record Keeping

Joone will maintain all records related to customer identification and transactions for a period as required by local law. These records will be made available to relevant authorities upon request.

Training and Awareness

Joone will provide regular training to all employees regarding AML/CTF policies and procedures. This will help ensure that all staff understand their responsibilities and are aware of the procedures to detect, prevent, and report money laundering and terrorist financing.

Compliance Officer

Joone will designate a Compliance Officer responsible for the oversight and execution of the AML/CTF program. The Compliance Officer will have the authority to enforce the company's AML/CT```html F policy and ensure the company's adherence to all applicable laws and regulations.

Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed at least annually or as required by changes in legislation or regulations. Any updates to this policy will be communicated to all relevant persons within Joone.

Feedback

Joone welcomes feedback on the implementation of our AML/CTF policy. Please let Joone know if users have any questions or suggestions at hello@joone.org.

5. COOKIES

Joone uses cookies to understand and save users' preferences for future visits and compile aggregate data about site traffic and site interaction so that Joone can offer better site experiences and tools in the future. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but if users prefer, they can edit their browser options to block them in the future.

5.1 What are cookies?

A cookie is a small file placed on users' computer's hard drive when they visit a website. It enables the website to remember users' actions and preferences over a period of time, so they don't have to keep re-entering them whenever they come back to the site or browse from one page to another.

5.2 How Joone uses cookies

Joone uses cookies to understand and save users' preferences for future visits and compile aggregate data about site traffic and site interaction so that Joone can offer better site experiences and tools in the future.

5.3 Users' choices

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but if users prefer, they can edit their browser options to block them in the future. The Help portion of the toolbar on most browsers will tell users how to prevent their computer from accepting new cookies, how to have the browser notify them when they receive a new cookie, or how to disable cookies altogether.
